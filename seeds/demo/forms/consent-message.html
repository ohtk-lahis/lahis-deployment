Privacy notice

LAHIS demo — for local testing and training

This notice explains how the LAHIS / One Health Toolkit mobile app collects and uses personal information when you register and submit animal health reports.

Information we collect

1. Account details you provide at registration (for example name, telephone, email, village, and optional gender or age when required by your organisation).

2. Report and census data you submit through the app (animal health observations, locations, photos, and related form answers).

3. Technical information needed to keep the service secure (device identifiers used for notifications, and authentication tokens).

How we use the information

1. To create and manage your reporter account and village assignment.

2. To receive, review, and respond to animal health reports and related follow-up work.

3. To produce aggregated statistics and situation summaries for authorised One Health staff.

4. We use only what is necessary for these purposes. We do not sell your personal information.

Sharing and protection

1. Information may be shared with authorised officers and partner organisations that operate this surveillance system for your area.

2. Access is limited to people who need it for their role. Communication with the server uses encrypted transport (HTTPS).

3. If external service providers process data on our behalf, they must protect it and must not use it for other purposes.

Your agreement

By accepting this notice you confirm that you understand how your information is used for community animal health reporting in this demo environment. Contact your local system administrator if you have questions about this policy.
